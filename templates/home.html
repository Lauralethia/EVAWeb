Are We Ready for Real-world Neuroscience?

Relevance of Ecological Validity

Ecological Validity Assessment (EVA) Tool

Pre-release vertion, December 2020

In cognitive neurosciences, fundamental principles of perception and functional brain organization have been established with highly controlled tasks and testing environments. To investigate cognitive functions and their underlying brain mechanisms in naturalistic settings, recent technical advances also allow for real-world neuroscience approaches. The diversity in approaches can be classified by a three-stage cycle which includes controlled laboratory research, partially naturalistic laboratory research approach, and naturalistic real-world research approach. Each of these approaches yields a different degree of ecological validity which is important to consider when comparing results across studies, and identifying gaps for future research.

Based on this three-stage cycle, we developed the Ecological Validity Assessment (EVA) tool to inform about the approach a researcher has taken in a short and easy manner. It allows researchers to objectively describe the ecological validity of their research project and identify where it fits on the continuum ranging from laboratory research to real-world, naturalistic research. The EVA tool consists of a set of ten questions concerning the specifics of a study. It outputs a summary of the provided answers as well as a compass plot which represents the studyâ€™s level of ecological validity which can easily be used for pre-registration, grant proposals, papers, reviews and meta-analyses. It would improve drawing conclusions across studies, and raising awareness for the validity and applicability of studies.
