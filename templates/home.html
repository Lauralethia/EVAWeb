Are We Ready for Real-world Neuroscience?

Relevance of Ecological Validity

Ecological Validity Assessment (EVA) Tool

Pre-release vertion, May 2020

The consideration of ecological validity while designing a study is one of the main challenges in cognitive neuroscience. Most of the studies typically include highly controlled tasks as well as artificial testing environments which leads to tremendous decreases in ecological validity. Recent technical advances allow for improvements in study design which is why many cognitive neuroscience studies have started working towards increasing ecological validity of their tasks. What would help is to objectively assess and report the level of ecological validity of one’s study. Such assessment would improve comparing results of different studies, drawing conclusions based on different studies, and identifying gaps for future research increasing the overall validity and applicability of studies.

Thus, we developed the Ecological Validity Assessment (EVA) tool to assess ecological validity of psychological and neuroscientific studies. It allows researchers to objectively describe the ecological validity of their research project and identify where it fits on the continuum ranging from traditional laboratory research to real-world, fully naturalistic research.

The EVA tool consists of a set of ten questions concerning the specifics of a study. It outputs a summary of the provided answers as well as a compass plot which represents the study’s level of ecological validity which can easily be used for pre-registration, grant proposals, papers, reviews and meta-analyses.
